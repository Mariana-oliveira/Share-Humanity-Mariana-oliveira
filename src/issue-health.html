Health

More than 5 million children die before their fifth birthday each year

Know how to help

Facts

  • Children born into poverty are almost twice as likely to die before the age of five as those from wealthier families.
  • 77.3 million people have become infected with HIV since the start of the epidemic.
  • 35.4 million people have died from AIDS-related illnesses since the start of the epidemic.
  • Tuberculosis remains the leading cause of death among people living with HIV.
  • In Eastern Asia, Northern Africa and Southern Asia, maternal mortality has declined by around two-thirds.
  • The global malaria incidence rate has fallen by an estimated 37% and the mortality rates by 58%.

This info is provided by United Nations.

How to Help

Here you can find different entities and communities that you can join as volunteer to help on this fight against health.

FIMRC

Foundation for International Medical Relief of Children is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the health of families in the developing world.

They work in 9 different countries, to provide access to healthcare for low-resource and medically underserved families around the world.

Visit →

HVO

Health Volunteers Overseas projects are staffed by highly qualified health care professionals.

Improves the availability and quality of health care through the education, training and professional development of the health workforce> in resource-scarce countries.

Visit →

GVI

Help communities gain access to clean water, sanitation and information on a variety of health topics such as maternal health, HIV/AIDS awareness and basic nutrition.

GVI runs programs in 21 locations, in 13 countries around the world.
